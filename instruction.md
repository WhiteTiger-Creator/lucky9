Evaluate the Meridian-2 network transport observable for a directed flow network and report the sustained flux.

Meridian-2 is a directed flow network from the statistical physics of transport: flux enters at a single source and is carried downstream along vertex-disjoint transport channels. The physical observable of interest is the maximum flux the network can sustain simultaneously — because channels contend for sites, a site carried by one channel is unavailable to another, so the sustained flux is the maximum over vertex-disjoint channel sets, deliberately **not** the sum of each downstream site's strongest channel (that over-counts shared sites) and **not** a greedy commit-the-strongest-channel value (greedy does not realise the maximum). `/app/docs/model_spec.md` defines the network format, the conditioning rules, the channel definition and span bound, the sustained-flux observable, and the exact output schema and checksum serialization. Read it closely — the observable is the exact maximum over vertex-disjoint channels, and both the per-site sum and the greedy value are wrong answers.

Given the network in `/app/data/network.json`, evaluate the transport and record the results in `result.json`: the reachable sites, the strongest single channel and its flux, the sustained flux `max_flux`, and the `edge_checksum` and `flux_checksum` over the exact serializations the spec defines.

Carry out the computation in `/app/flux.py`, reading `--input` (default `/app/data/network.json`) and writing under `--output-dir` (default `/app/output`), and run it so `/app/output/result.json` is in place. Derive every value from the input; don't read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
