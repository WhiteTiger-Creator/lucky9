Simulate the Meridian-2 SE-draining chip-firing model and report its stabilized state. Chip-firing — the abelian sandpile — is a discrete dynamical system from statistical physics and combinatorics: chips are distributed on a grid and unstable sites topple until the configuration stabilizes. The Meridian-2 variant is a *directed, order-sensitive* model whose toppling rule and firing schedule `/app/docs/model_spec.md` defines precisely. Unlike the classical sandpile it is **not** order-independent — the final grid depends on the schedule in which cells are fired, so that schedule is part of the model.

Given the initial chip drops in `/app/data/drops.json`, evolve the grid to its stabilized configuration under the Meridian-2 model and record the results in `result.json`: the stabilized grid, the total and per-row firing counts, the `spill` of chips that leave the grid, and a `grid_checksum` over the exact serialization the model defines. The simulation runs from `/app/stabilize.py`, which presently evolves the classical four-neighbour rule; make it evolve the Meridian-2 model instead. It exposes `--input` and `--output-dir` flags (defaults `/app/data/drops.json` and `/app/output`); keep them.

Run it so `/app/output/result.json` is in place. Derive everything from the input; don't read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
