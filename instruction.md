Host process-execution monitoring is an endpoint security control, and during the Warden security incident the containment rollup that decides which executions require responder isolation began producing an untrustworthy queue, leaving malicious processes uncontained. The restored system must reflect the execution-review board's security rulings exactly. A reconciler at /app/workflow/export_report.py must turn the raw execution events into the containment artifacts summary.json, host_matrix.json and contained.jsonl that responders act on, and an audit tool at /app/exec_audit.py must offer two subcommands, diagnose and repair: diagnose emits a stateless report of the six known containment defects with their evidence, and repair rebuilds the rollup, runs it, and writes diagnosis.json and repair_audit.json beside the three artifacts, defaulting to /app/output. The board's security decisions behind every value are recorded in the dossier at /app/incident/exec_review_dossier.md, the authoritative record where a later dated ruling overrides an earlier one, and the exact schemas, key sets, field types, digest payloads and checksum serialization are fixed by the contract at /app/docs/report_spec.json. The read-only frozen snapshot at /app/workflow/.export_report.original must remain unmodified, and every emitted artifact must validate against the contract.
