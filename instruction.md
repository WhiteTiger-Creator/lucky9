Simulate the Meridian-2 SE-draining chip-firing model and compute its stabilized state.

Chip-firing — the abelian sandpile — is a discrete dynamical system from statistical physics and combinatorics: chips sit on a grid, and any site holding too many chips *topples*, redistributing chips to its neighbours, until the field stabilizes. Meridian-2 is a *directed, order-sensitive* variant. Its toppling rule and firing schedule are defined precisely in `/app/docs/model_spec.md`. Unlike the classical sandpile it is **not** order-independent: the stabilized field depends on the schedule in which sites are fired, so that schedule is part of the model. The surge term and the mandated firing order are what make the result well-defined — read the spec closely rather than assuming the textbook four-neighbour rule.

Given the initial chip drops in `/app/data/drops.json`, evolve the field to its stabilized configuration under Meridian-2 and record the results in `result.json`: the stabilized grid, the total and per-row firing counts, the `spill` of chips that leave the grid, and a `grid_checksum` over the exact serialization the spec defines.

Write your simulation as `/app/stabilize.py`, exposing `--input` (default `/app/data/drops.json`) and `--output-dir` (default `/app/output`), and run it so `/app/output/result.json` is in place. Derive everything from the input; don't read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
