Numerically simulate the Meridian-2 directed lattice-relaxation model and report its steady state.

Meridian-2 is a discrete-time dynamical system on a two-dimensional integer lattice — a directed variant of the chip-firing / abelian-sandpile family from statistical physics and combinatorics. Load accumulates at lattice sites; wherever a site is overloaded it relaxes, transporting load to its south and east neighbours, until the field converges to a stable steady state. The full dynamics — the relaxation rule, the surge term, and the mandated relaxation schedule — are defined in `/app/docs/model_spec.md`. Unlike the classical abelian sandpile the model is **not** order-independent: the steady state depends on the schedule in which sites relax, so that schedule is part of the physics. Read the spec closely rather than assuming the textbook four-neighbour toppling rule.

From the initial load in `/app/data/drops.json`, evolve the lattice to its steady state under Meridian-2 and record the observables in `result.json`: the steady-state lattice, the total and per-row relaxation-event counts, the `spill` of load that leaves the domain, and a `grid_checksum` over the exact serialization the spec defines.

Implement the simulation as `/app/stabilize.py`, reading `--input` (default `/app/data/drops.json`) and writing under `--output-dir` (default `/app/output`), and run it so `/app/output/result.json` is in place. Derive every observable from the initial load; don't read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
