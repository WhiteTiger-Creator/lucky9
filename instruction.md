Simulate the Meridian SE-draining chip-firing model and report its stabilized state. Chip-firing — the abelian sandpile — is a discrete dynamical system studied in statistical physics and combinatorics: chips are distributed across the sites of a grid, and any site holding too many chips topples, redistributing chips to other sites, until the configuration reaches its unique stable state. The Meridian variant is a *directed* model whose toppling rule `/app/docs/model_spec.md` defines precisely; it is not the classical four-neighbour sandpile, and the two evolve to different stable states.

Given the initial chip drops in `/app/data/drops.json`, evolve the grid to its unique stable configuration under the Meridian rule and record the results in `result.json`: the stabilized grid, the total and per-row firing counts, the `spill` of chips that leave the grid, and a `grid_checksum` over the exact serialization the model defines. The simulation runs from `/app/stabilize.py`, which presently evolves the classical four-neighbour rule; make it evolve the Meridian model instead. It exposes `--input` and `--output-dir` flags (defaults `/app/data/drops.json` and `/app/output`); keep them.

Run it so `/app/output/result.json` is in place. Derive everything from the input; don't read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
