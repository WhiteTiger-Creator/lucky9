Numerically simulate the Meridian-2 directed lattice-relaxation model and report its steady state.

Meridian-2 is a discrete-time dynamical system on a two-dimensional integer lattice — a directed variant of the chip-firing / abelian-sandpile family from statistical physics and combinatorics. Load accumulates at lattice sites; wherever a site is overloaded it relaxes, transporting load to its south and east neighbours, until the field converges to a stable steady state. Unlike the classical abelian sandpile the model is **not** order-independent: the steady state depends on the schedule in which sites relax.

`/app/docs/model_spec.md` defines the *form* of the model — the relaxation rule, the conservation invariant, and the exact output contract — but it deliberately does **not** fix the numeric coefficients or the relaxation schedule. Those are calibrated constants: the overload threshold, the surge divisor, the base removal, the eastward and southward fluxes, and the schedule that selects which site relaxes next. The authoritative record of their calibration is `/app/docs/calibration_notebook.md`. It is a working notebook — the early February entries are first-pass estimates and several were revised in the May recalibration; most of it is routine run logging. Read it as an account that resolves over time: where a draft estimate and a later recalibration disagree, the later recalibration governs. Recover the calibrated constants and schedule that survive reconciliation — a single wrong constant or the wrong schedule yields the wrong steady state.

From the initial load in `/app/data/drops.json`, evolve the lattice to its steady state under the reconciled model and record the observables in `result.json`: the steady-state lattice, the total and per-row relaxation-event counts, the `spill` of load that leaves the domain, and a `grid_checksum` over the exact serialization the contract defines.

Implement the simulation as `/app/stabilize.py`, reading `--input` (default `/app/data/drops.json`) and writing under `--output-dir` (default `/app/output`), and run it so `/app/output/result.json` is in place. Derive every observable from the initial load; don't read or import anything from `/tests` or `/solution`.
